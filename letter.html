Letter

Regine P. Sumagaysay
Baribi Sasa
Hinatuan, Surigao Del Sur
5/25/2025

Dear Future Guardians of the Earth,

In a decade and a half, I envision a world where harsh climates are no longer a threat. I see thriving green jungles, pristine beaches, and communities powered solely by wind, sunlight, and plant-based energy. By then, I hope that the responsibilities we once neglected will have been embraced—leading to cleaner environments, better health, and renewed protection for wildlife.

I dream of a time when majestic species, especially those under threat like elephants, are no longer hunted but safeguarded with pride. Their survival depends on the choices we make today—shifting away from destruction and moving toward sustainability with compassion and purpose.

To nature—we thank you deeply for your beauty, your gifts, and your resilience. It is our solemn promise to protect and preserve you, not just for ourselves but for the grandchildren we may never meet.

With hope and responsibility,
Regine P. Sumagaysay

Dear Mother Nature,

I just want to express my deepest thanks to the Forest Guardians who dedicate their time and energy to protecting your forests. Their love and commitment to nature are truly inspiring. Because of their hard work, your ecosystems stay in balance, and the animals and plants that call the forest home are kept safe. What they do reminds all of us how important it is to care for our planet.

I hope you continue to bless and guide them in everything they do. May their passion inspire more people to stand up for nature and be part of the change.

With sincere gratitude,
Supremo, Igrace G.

Dear Mother Nature,

Thank you for the life you give us every single day.

Thank you for the soft breeze that cools our skin, the warm sun that lights our path, and the rains that bring your blessings to the earth. Thank you for the tall trees that stand like guardians, the flowers that bloom with joy, and the animals that share this planet with us.

You are the heart of the world—the source of all beauty, balance, and wonder.

We are grateful for the food you grow, the water you provide, and the peace you offer in your quiet places. You fill our world with color, sound, and life.

Even though we sometimes forget to take care of you, you continue to give. We are sorry for the times we have been careless, and we promise to do better—for you, for ourselves, and for the future.

Thank you for your endless gifts, your strength, and your love.

With deep respect and gratitude,
Prestoza, Marco D.

Dear Mother Nature,

Thank you—truly, from the bottom of our hearts.

Thank you for the sunshine that brightens our days, and the rain that helps our food grow. Thank you for the fresh air we breathe, the trees that give us shade, and the rivers, seas, and lakes that cool and calm us.

Thank you for the mountains that stand strong, the flowers that bloom with color, and the animals that remind us of the beauty of life.

You give without asking for anything in return. You heal even when we hurt you. You remain beautiful, even when we forget to appreciate you.

We may not always show it, but we are deeply grateful for all that you do. Without you, there would be no life. You are our home, our provider, and our greatest teacher.

We promise to love and care for you better. To protect your forests, clean your waters, and respect all your living creatures. You deserve our care, just as you have always cared for us.

Thank you, Mother Nature. We are forever thankful for your love.

Calnea Cindy C.

Dear Forest Guardian,

Thank you for taking care of the forest. Because of you, the trees, animals, and clean air are safe. You help the Earth stay healthy and green.

We know your work is not easy. You walk in the forest, plant trees, and protect animals. You also stop people from hurting the forest. That is a big job, and we are thankful for what you do.

The forest is home to many animals. It gives us fresh air, clean water, and food. Without the forest, life would be hard. That is why your job is very important.

Even if no one sees you working, we know you are a hero. You help the world in a quiet but strong way. Please don’t stop. We believe in you.

Thank you for your kind heart. Thank you for being a protector of nature.

Sincerely,
Valencia, Jelyn S.

Dear Future Protectors of the Earth,

I imagine a world where extreme weather is no longer a danger. I see lush green forests, clean beaches, and towns that run on wind, sunlight, and plant power. By then, I hope we’ve taken better care of our planet—making it cleaner, healthier, and safer for all living things.

I dream of a time when animals like elephants are no longer hunted but protected with love and respect. Their future depends on the choices we make now—choosing kindness and sustainability instead of harm.

To nature—thank you for your beauty, your gifts, and your strength.

We promise to care for you, not just for ourselves, but for future generations.


Sincerely,

Aloyon, Ralph Moses E.
